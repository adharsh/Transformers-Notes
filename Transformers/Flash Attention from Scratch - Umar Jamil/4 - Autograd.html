Why Are There Zero Entries?

The zero entries arise because each output yijy_{ij}yij​ depends only on the row iii of xxx and not on the other rows. For example:

  • y11y_{11}y11​ depends only on x11x_{11}x11​ and x12x_{12}x12​, so derivatives w.r.t. x21x_{21}x21​ and x22x_{22}x22​ are zero.
  • Similarly, y21y_{21}y21​ depends only on x21x_{21}x21​ and x22x_{22}x22​, so derivatives w.r.t. x11x_{11}x11​ and x12x_{12}x12​ are zero.

This pattern of dependency leads to a block-diagonal structure, making the Jacobian very sparse.
