How to write a numerically stable softmax or a safe softmax?

Given a vector x∈RNx \in \mathbb{R}^Nx∈RN, the softmax is defined as:

softmax(xi)=exi∑j=1Nexj\text{softmax}(x_i) = \dfrac{e^{x_i}}{\sum_{j=1}^{N} e^{x_j}} softmax(xi​)=∑j=1N​exj​exi​​

But there's a problem! If the values of the vector are large, the exponential will explode!

Numerically unstable: cannot be represented with a float 32 or float 16.

Luckily, we have a solution:

exi∑j=1Nexj=c⋅exi∑j=1Nc⋅exj=elog⁡(c)⋅exi∑j=1Nelog⁡(c)⋅exj=elog⁡(c)+xi∑j=1Nelog⁡(c)+xj\dfrac{e^{x_i}}{\sum_{j=1}^N e^{x_j}} = \dfrac{c \cdot e^{x_i}}{\sum_{j=1}^N c \cdot e^{x_j}} = \dfrac{e^{\log(c)} \cdot e^{x_i}}{\sum_{j=1}^N e^{\log(c)} \cdot e^{x_j}} = \dfrac{e^{\log(c) + x_i}}{\sum_{j=1}^N e^{\log(c) + x_j}} ∑j=1N​exj​exi​​=∑j=1N​c⋅exj​c⋅exi​​=∑j=1N​elog(c)⋅exj​elog(c)⋅exi​​=∑j=1N​elog(c)+xj​elog(c)+xi​​

=exi+log⁡(c)∑j=1Nexj+log⁡(c)=exi−k∑j=1Nexj−k= \dfrac{e^{x_i + \log(c)}}{\sum_{j=1}^N e^{x_j + \log(c)}} = \dfrac{e^{x_i - k}}{\sum_{j=1}^N e^{x_j - k}} =∑j=1N​exj​+log(c)exi​+log(c)​=∑j=1N​exj​−kexi​−k​

Where k=−log⁡(c)k = -\log(c)k=−log(c).

So we can "sneak in" a constant in the exponential to decrease its argument and make it numerically stable.

When x≤0x \leq 0x≤0, ex≤1e^x \leq 1ex≤1 and is numerically stable.
When x>0x > 0x>0, exe^xex can be large and numerically unstable.
